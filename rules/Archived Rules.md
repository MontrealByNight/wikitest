
## Expanded Ancillae character creation rules

An Ancillae can use the advanced rules as they are in the _"In Memoriam"_ Rulebook, but those rules have been slightly updated to reflect our server and the game in a play by post setting. Those rules are more complicated and while they might offer some advantages compared to the standard character creation offered in the corebook, the backstory need to be adequate to reflect the different _"Eras"_ lived by the character and any attempt to powerplay through those will be dealt with accordingly.

Moreover, we don't use a random generation of the flaws, you simply pick which ones you want.&#x20;

{% hint style="danger" %}
_**Age, eras and predator type, combined, CANNOT bring humanity at a score lower than 4 and if they do, they can't be picked.**_
{% endhint %}

## Attributes and abilites:

The same as the normal character creation.

## Discipline:

You can choose between Focused or Strategic.

* _Focused_: Put 3 dots into a clan discipline, 1 dot into another clan discipline and the last 1 dot into any discipline. (with the usual limitations). Caitiff characters will have those 3 as their starting disciplines.
* _Strategic_: Put 2 dots into 2 clan disciplines, 1 dot into the third clan discipline and then 1 dot into any discipline (with the usual limitations).

## Convictions, Touchstone, Chronicle Tenets and Humanity

The same as the normal character creation. Before the Predator Type, the Generation and the Eras, your starting humanity is 7.

## Predator type:

The same as the normal character creation.

## Merits, flaws and backgrounds.

They are fully dependant on your Generation and the Eras.

## Generation:

This will massively change the rest of the sheet, so pick carefully. It will also influence your blood potency and your starting advantages and flaws.

* _12th generation_ - You will have Blood Potency 1

> While your blood is weaker than many of your contemporaries, you make up for it with mortal connection and acumen. You have **8 dots of Advantages** and **12 experience points to spend on skills**.

* _11th or 10th generation_ - You will have Blood Potency 2

> Like most ancilla, you are a cut above the average neonate in therms of power, but years of unlife have left their mark in the form of regrets, disadvantages and enemies. You have **8 dots of Advantages** and **3 dots of flaws**.

* _9th or 8th generation_ - You will have Blood Potency 3

> The strength of your Blood is almost on par with an elder, but the challenge of wielding it has led you down many dark roads, and your history is haunted by tragedy. You have **5 dots of flaws**. You may take **2 dots of Advantages** at the cost of **a permanent loss of 1 dot of Humanity**.

## Oceans of time

Depending on your backstory and your generation, your character will have gone through different Eras in their unlife. Those might be events in their city, in their country, in their court, etcetera. They determine what your kindred has done in those centuries and it shapes their personality, history and sheet.

* For Ancillae Embraced up to 100 years ago, choose **3 Eras**. You will receive the Flaws of **1** of those. (This is recommended to Ancillae of the 12th Generation)
* For Ancillae embraced up to 150 years ago, choose **4 Eras**. You will receive the Flaws of **2** of those. (This is recommended to Ancillae of the 11th or 10th generation). Also, lose **1 permanent dot of Humanity**
* For Ancillae older than that, choose **5 Eras**. You will receive the Flaws of **3** of those. (This is recommended to Ancillae of the 9th or 8th Generation). Also, lose **2 permanent dots of Humanity**

## Eras

Those are the eras available. Those must be included in your backstory and it needs to make sense. All of those bonuses and maluses are cumulative with the Generation and the Predator type. Age, eras and predator type, combined, CANNOT bring humanity at a score lower than 4 and if they do, they can't be picked.

* A TIME OF ADVERSITY: You spent more nights than you would've preferred huddled up in damp cellars, licking your wounds and eyeing the rats. Gain **skills worth 12 experience points** one suitable **Archaic Skill specialty**. You also gain **2 dots of Background flaws**.

> _Flaw:_ You get **10 experience** points to spend on Disciplines and **lose 1 Humanity.**

* A TIME OF CALM: It went by much too quickly, but for a while, your nights were mostly free of dangers. You immersed yourself in your passions and grew your abilities. Gain **skills worth 6 experience points** and one suitable **Archaic Skill specialty**.

> _Flaw:_ You gain another \*\*3 experience points and **one dot in Antiquated Flaws**.

* A TIME OF INTRIGUE: As others jealously reached for your assets, you had to be clever to protect yourself. Gain **two dots of Background Advantages** and one suitable **Archaic Skill specialty**.

> _Flaw:_ You gain another **two dots of Background Advantages** and **lose 1 Humanity.**

* A TIME OF EXCESS: The consummate leech, you indulged in every vice and even managed to overcome some of them. Gain **three dots of Advantages** to spend on **Bonding and Feeding Merits.**

> \*Flaw: You gain **10 experience points to spend** on Disciplines. You also gain **three dots of Supernatural or Substance Use Flaws** _or_ **lose 1 Humanity**.

* A TIME OF VIOLENCE: You indulged your Beast, letting it loose upon your enemies and any others who were in the way. **Lose 1 Humanity** and gain **15 experience points** to spend on any Disciplines. This era can only be chosen once.

> _Flaw_: You gain the **Dark Secret Flaw (●)**.

* A TIME OF SORCERY: You turned your mind to the sorcerous side of undead existence, finding secrets in the Blood. **Lose 1 Humanity** and gain **15 experience points** to spend on either Mental Attributes, Occult, or Blood Sorcery or Oblivion. This era can only be chosen once.

> _Flaw_: You gain another 3 experience points to spend on the above, you also gain a **Cursed Object (•) Flaw**.

* TORPOR: You spent an era in torpor, hoping time would erase some of your mistakes. Remove **one dot of Flaws**.
